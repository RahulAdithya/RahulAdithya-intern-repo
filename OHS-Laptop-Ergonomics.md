### 1. Risks of Direct Laptop Use
Using a laptop on a flat desk without external peripherals often leads to "Laptop Hunch." Key risks include:
* **Cervical Strain (Text Neck):** Looking down for long periods puts immense pressure on the neck and spine.
* **Repetitive Strain Injury (RSI):** The cramped layout of laptop keyboards and trackpads can cause wrist and finger discomfort.
* **Eye Strain:** Fixed focal distances and screen glare can lead to headaches and blurred vision.

### 2. Ergonomic Equipment Solutions
* **Laptop Stand:** Raises the screen to eye level to prevent neck slouching.
* **External Keyboard & Mouse:** Allows your elbows to remain at a 90-degree angle while your shoulders stay relaxed.
* **Ergonomic Chair:** Provides lumbar support to maintain the natural curve of the spine.



### 3. Ideal Setup Adjustments
* **Monitor Height:** The top third of the screen should be at eye level.
* **Chair Position:** Feet should be flat on the floor with knees at a 90-degree angle.
* **Desk Height:** Your forearms should be parallel to the floor when typing.

### 4. Healthy Daily Habits
* **20-20-20 Rule:** Every 20 minutes, look at something 20 feet away for 20 seconds to reduce eye strain.
* **Movement Breaks:** Standing up and stretching every hour to improve circulation.
* **Posture Checks:** Frequently checking if shoulders are hunched toward the ears.
